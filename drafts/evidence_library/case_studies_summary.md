# Case Studies Summary

We have successfully completed hundreds of projects for a wide range of clients in the residential, commercial, and infrastructure sectors. Summaries of three comparable projects are provided below. Full case studies are available upon request.

---
### Case Study 1: Urban Residential Development, Anytown

- **Site type:** Complex urban, multi-period archaeology.
- **Context:** Large-scale residential development on a historically significant site.
- **Objectives:** Evaluation, full excavation, and mitigation ahead of construction.
- **Methods:** Trial trenching, open-area excavation, geoarchaeology, and specialist finds analysis.
- **Constraints:** Aggressive construction programme, deep stratigraphy, and public engagement requirements.
- **Outcomes:** The site was successfully cleared for development on schedule. A major report was published, and the finds archive was deposited with the local museum.

---
### Case Study 2: Infrastructure Pipeline, County Lines

- **Site type:** Linear infrastructure project across a rural landscape.
- **Context:** 25km water pipeline scheme.
- **Objectives:** To assess and mitigate the archaeological impact along the entire route.
- **Methods:** Desk-based assessment, geophysical survey, and a rolling programme of watching brief and targeted excavation.
- **Constraints:** Restricted working corridor, multiple land ownerships, and seasonal ecological constraints.
- **Outcomes:** All archaeological requirements were discharged without delaying the principal contractor. Several previously unknown sites were identified and recorded.

---
### Case Study 3: Heritage Asset Management, Historic Estate

- **Site type:** Designated heritage assets within a listed park and garden.
- **Context:** Conservation and development management for a historic estate.
- **Objectives:** To provide expert heritage advice and undertake conservation-led investigation.
- **Methods:** Heritage statements, conservation management planning, standing building recording, and watching brief during repairs.
- **Constraints:** Working within a highly sensitive historic fabric with a need for minimal intervention.
- **Outcomes:** Enabled the client to secure planning and listed building consent for works while enhancing the understanding and conservation of the heritage assets.
