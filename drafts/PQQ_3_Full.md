### Q: - Field Archaeology

# Fieldwork & Site Investigation Services

Our experienced field team is equipped to undertake the full range of archaeological site investigations, from initial appraisal to full excavation.

*   **Geophysical Survey:** Non-intrusive survey (magnetometry, resistivity) to identify subsurface anomalies.
*   **Trial Trench Evaluation:** Targeted machine-dug trenching to determine the character, extent, and significance of archaeological remains.
*   **Archaeological Excavation:** Controlled excavation and recording of archaeological deposits to mitigate the impact of development.
*   **Watching Brief (Archaeological Observation):** Monitoring of groundworks in areas of archaeological potential to identify and record any finds or features.

All fieldwork is conducted using our in-house survey equipment and is compliant with CIfA standards and our rigorous Health & Safety protocols.

### Q: - Heritage Consultancy

# Planning & Heritage Consultancy Services

We provide a full suite of services to guide clients through the planning process, ensuring heritage assets are properly considered and managed.

*   **Desk-Based Assessments (DBA):** Comprehensive baseline assessments of known and potential heritage assets to inform on development risk and opportunities.
*   **Heritage Statements / Statements of Significance:** Detailed assessments to accompany planning applications affecting designated heritage assets (e.g., Listed Buildings, Conservation Areas).
*   **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA):** Production of Cultural Heritage chapters for Environmental Statements.
*   **Written Schemes of Investigation (WSI):** Designing and agreeing on detailed project methodologies with Local Planning Authority archaeologists.
*   **Discharge of Planning Conditions:** Providing the necessary archaeological works and reporting to discharge planning conditions.

### Q: - Post-Excavation

# Post-Excavation & Analysis

Our post-excavation department manages the processing, analysis, and reporting of all data and artefacts recovered during fieldwork.

*   **Finds Processing:** Cleaning, marking, and cataloguing of all recovered artefacts in accordance with museum standards.
*   **Specialist Analysis:** Coordination and management of specialist assessment for various material types, including:
    *   Pottery
    *   Human and Animal Bone
    *   Environmental Samples (e.g., pollen, charcoal)
    *   Lithics and Small Finds
*   **Reporting:** Production of high-quality, illustrated reports (grey literature) suitable for submission to the client and the local Historic Environment Record (HER).
*   **Publication & Dissemination:** Preparation of findings for publication in academic journals and popular articles.
*   **Archive Preparation:** Compilation and deposition of the project archive with a recognised museum or repository.

# Case Studies Summary

We have successfully completed hundreds of projects for a wide range of clients in the residential, commercial, and infrastructure sectors. Summaries of three comparable projects are provided below. Full case studies are available upon request.

---
### Case Study 1: Urban Residential Development, Anytown

- **Site type:** Complex urban, multi-period archaeology.
- **Context:** Large-scale residential development on a historically significant site.
- **Objectives:** Evaluation, full excavation, and mitigation ahead of construction.
- **Methods:** Trial trenching, open-area excavation, geoarchaeology, and specialist finds analysis.
- **Constraints:** Aggressive construction programme, deep stratigraphy, and public engagement requirements.
- **Outcomes:** The site was successfully cleared for development on schedule. A major report was published, and the finds archive was deposited with the local museum.

---
### Case Study 2: Infrastructure Pipeline, County Lines

- **Site type:** Linear infrastructure project across a rural landscape.
- **Context:** 25km water pipeline scheme.
- **Objectives:** To assess and mitigate the archaeological impact along the entire route.
- **Methods:** Desk-based assessment, geophysical survey, and a rolling programme of watching brief and targeted excavation.
- **Constraints:** Restricted working corridor, multiple land ownerships, and seasonal ecological constraints.
- **Outcomes:** All archaeological requirements were discharged without delaying the principal contractor. Several previously unknown sites were identified and recorded.
